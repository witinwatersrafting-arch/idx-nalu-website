Which season should I come to the Nile? (我應該在哪個季節來尼羅河？)
Water Levels:
The water levels here are very stable year-round because there is a dam built just after the Nile flows out of Lake Victoria. According to an agreement between the countries the Nile flows through, Lake Victoria must release a minimum flow of water to allow the Nile to flow into Egypt. This minimum flow creates excellent kayaking conditions; if the dam does release more water for a few days, it remains a fantastic kayaking experience. As power demands change from day to night (thereby affecting the amount of water released), the water levels fluctuate daily, but there is always flow here to paddle and surf. Generally, we have lower water levels in the morning and higher levels in the afternoon.

Seasons:
In recent years, the rainy seasons seem to have become a bit more unpredictable, but generally, the rainy season occurs twice a year: once from April to May, and another from October to December. Having said that, the sun still shines brightly during the rainy season, and it also still rains during the dry season! The rainy season doesn't actually affect the river itself, but it might make the journey to the river slightly more difficult as the roads can get quite muddy. But one thing is for sure here: the weather is always warm!

Are there crocodiles or hippos? (有鱷魚或河馬嗎？)
In the section of the Nile where we kayak (from Jinja to Busana), there are no hippos, and 99% of the crocodiles have been wiped out. Prior to Idi Amin's rule in the 1970s, these animals were abundant in the area, but since then, they have been completely poached out.
After more than 20 years of rafting and kayaking activities, and hundreds of thousands of customers having fun on the Nile, there has never been a crocodile-related incident here.
If you want to see these animals on the Nile, you will need to drive 8 hours to Murchison Falls National Park.

Is Uganda safe? (烏干達安全嗎？)
Uganda is one of the friendliest and most peaceful countries in Africa. There has been unrest in the far north of the country in the past, and to a lesser extent in the far west in recent times, but overall, Uganda is very stable as a country.
Petty and opportunistic crimes do occasionally happen, but serious crimes are extremely rare. As long as you exercise good travel common sense and keep your belongings secure, the risk of becoming a victim of crime is very low.

Will I get bilharzia/schistosomiasis in the Nile? (我會在尼羅河感染血吸蟲病嗎？)
It is widely known that the Nile carries bilharzia pathogens, and there are documented cases of infections from the river.
Bilharzia is most prevalent in still and stagnant water. We spend most of our time in fast-flowing rapids, or on sections of the river where the water level fluctuates wildly every day, so these areas are essentially free of this parasite. To minimize the risk, we recommend not spending time wading near the riverbanks, and avoiding swimming in the stagnant waters of Lake Victoria.
Treatment medication (a full course of tablets) can be easily purchased at pharmacies here (in Jinja), but you must wait three months (the incubation period) before undergoing treatment. If you have concerns about bilharzia, we advise you to visit your doctor for a check-up three months after your trip, and they should be able to test for it.
For more information about bilharzia, please check the following resources:

NHS.uk – schistosomiasis (Bilharzia)

What is the current Ebola situation in Uganda? (烏干達的伊波拉疫情現況如何？)
We always advise that you follow the official advice from your home country regarding this issue. The following information is provided to serve as a guide for travelers.
Uganda was completely unaffected by the 2014 outbreak of the Zaire Ebola virus strain in West Africa. During this outbreak, Uganda recorded zero cases of that strain.
The 2022-2023 Ebola outbreak in Uganda was of the Sudan virus strain, occurring between September 20, 2022, and January 10, 2023, located in the western and central regions of Uganda. Over 160 people were infected; however, the disease has been successfully contained and eradicated.
During 2011 (1 confirmed case) and 2012 (31 confirmed cases), there were outbreaks of the Sudan strain of Ebola in the far north of Uganda. These outbreaks were successfully contained, and currently, Uganda is free of the Ebola virus, meaning the immediate risk is low.
Source – WHO Ebola Virus Disease factsheet no.103

9. Independent Kayakers (獨立玩家)
   Which season should I come to the Nile? (我應該在哪個季節來尼羅河？)
   Water Levels:
   The water levels here are very stable year-round because the Nile has a dam built just after flowing out of Lake Victoria. According to an agreement between the countries the Nile flows through, Lake Victoria must release a minimum flow of water to allow the Nile to flow into Egypt. This minimum flow creates excellent kayaking conditions; if the dam does release more water for a few days, it remains a fantastic kayaking experience. As power demands change from day to night (thereby affecting the amount of water released), the water levels fluctuate daily, but there is always flow here to paddle and surf. Generally, we have lower water levels in the morning and higher levels in the afternoon.

Seasons:
In recent years, the rainy seasons seem to have become a bit more unpredictable, but generally, the rainy season occurs twice a year: once from April to May, and another from October to December. Having said that, the sun still shines brightly during the rainy season, and it also still rains during the dry season! The rainy season doesn't actually affect the river itself, but it might make the journey to the river slightly more difficult as the roads can get quite muddy. But one thing is for sure here: the weather is always warm!

How long should I stay on the Nile? (我應該在尼羅河待多久？)
We recommend planning a trip to Uganda for at least two weeks, though people are often still surprised by how fast time flies when it's over. Two weeks is enough time for you to experience everything on the river once, and perhaps squeeze in a trip to Murchison or Sipi Falls.
Standard stay durations for whitewater kayakers coming here are two weeks, a month, and two months, sometimes even longer!

How do I handle meals if I stay in Bujagali village? (住在 Bujagali 村莊裡，三餐要怎麼解決？)
No need to worry at all! In fact, you can absolutely stay here for the entire month without ever needing the hassle of traveling into Jinja city to find food. In Bujagali, you can truly taste 100% authentic local flavors!

There are small diners opened by locals in the village, where a filling, authentic meal only costs about 4,000 UGX. You absolutely cannot miss the classic street national food, "Chapati Rolex" (a Ugandan specialty omelet wrap), priced between 1,000 and 4,000 UGX—guaranteed to be delicious and satisfying!

If you like to cook for yourself, small grocery stores can be seen everywhere in the village, stocking all daily ingredients like fresh vegetables and fruits, rice, oil, salt, garlic, onions, etc., with prices practically the same as in the city. If you want meat, just walk to the neighboring village where there are dedicated meat stalls; for those who love fish, you can even go fishing by the Nile yourself, or directly buy the freshest catch of the day from local fishermen. No matter how you want to eat, our local team is very happy to assist you anytime!

How do I commute to and from the river? (如何往返河流？)
When kayaking on the Nile, transport shuttling is a daily routine. No matter which campsite you choose to stay at, as long as you are heading out to challenge the main whitewater rapids (whether surfing or river running), you will need a car shuttle. But don't worry, this is very easy! The local shuttle system is very mature; you just need to clearly tell the driver your put-in and take-out points directly (for example: "Put in at Bujagali Dam, take out at Busowoko"), and it can be easily arranged.

Our exclusive highlight: Walk right to the river!
If you don't want to travel too far today and just want to find a calm, simple rapid to practice your feel, get used to the water flow, or try your hand at the most entry-level Grade 1 surf spot—good news! From our partnered village accommodation complex, you can walk directly to the river! You can easily put in and take out at a fixed spot without needing to spend any time or money arranging a shuttle; just carry your boat over and you can immediately enjoy the Nile!

To understand the locations of all put-ins and take-outs, please come and discuss with us upon arrival. Our guides are all kids who grew up on this river and can give you the best information.
