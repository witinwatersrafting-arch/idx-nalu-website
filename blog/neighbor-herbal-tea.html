Visiting My Fisherman Neighbor: The Most Authentic Nile Herbal Tea

Words: Wang Wanwan
Traveler

In the everyday life here in Bujagali, the most precious moments aren't always grand adventures, but unexpected local experiences.

One afternoon, I went to visit my neighbor, a local fisherman. He warmly welcomed me and invited me to join him for their daily "herbal tea".

I said yes! But to my surprise, instead of heading to the kitchen for a tea bag, he led me out of his yard, right to the roadside to freshly pick the herbs.

The Freshest Ingredients

In a Ugandan village, nature is the best refrigerator and pharmacy. We gathered fresh leaves and returned to his simple mud-walled home.

He expertly rubbed the freshly picked leaves before tossing them into a cast-iron kettle, letting it simmer slowly over a charcoal fire. The room soon filled with the smoky scent of firewood mixed with the refreshing fragrance of the herbs. Watching him patiently tend to the fire, the typical rush of tourists and itineraries completely lost all meaning here.

Only Real Locals Experience This

Once the water reached a rolling boil, he poured the yellow-green tea into three different colored plastic mugs. We sat on the dirt floor, sipping the hot, slightly bitter yet sweet-aftertaste local brew.

This isn't a "must-do" listed in any guidebook, and it doesn't cost a single dollar. But to me, this kind of raw, unfiltered local connection is the most fascinating part of deeply exploring Africa.
