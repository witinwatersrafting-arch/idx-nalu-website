Top Questions

Do you provide accommodation? ▼

Yes, we do. We have an exclusive partnered accommodation complex locally. This is a hidden gem truly integrated into the local village, and its location is absolutely excellent—it sits right on the mandatory daily route to the river, saving you valuable travel time.

To address the privacy and equipment protection that water sports enthusiasts care about the most, the complex features high surrounding walls and comprehensive security measures to guarantee absolute safety.

The accommodation offers comfortable en-suite bathrooms and comes with a spacious, private courtyard where you can wash and store all your kayaking equipment with complete peace of mind.

Additionally, having fires is permitted within the complex, and there is a dedicated BBQ area. This allows you to relax by the fire in a safe and private camp after a full day of whitewater challenges, enjoying the perfect time to rest and recover.

If you have no budget constraints, you might also consider the Nile River Explorers campsite. Accommodation options include camping, safari tents, multi-person dormitories, and private rooms. We also recommend checking out the newly opened Kalagala Site.

How should I pay? ▼

We try our best to make the payment process as simple as possible. Most people choose to pay in cash to avoid bank fees.

Currencies we accept:

  • Ugandan Shillings (UGX)
  • US Dollars (Unmarked and issues from 2009 onwards)
  • British Pounds (GBP)
  • Euros
What do I need to bring? ▼

To join our introduction to whitewater day trips and other activities, you only need to bring yourself along with:

  • Clothes you don't mind getting wet (shorts, T-shirt, swimsuit)
  • Any medication you might need (e.g., asthma inhaler, EpiPen). All guides carry a first aid kit on the water, and your medication can be kept inside it to stay dry and easily accessible.
  • Dry clothes to change into after the activity.

The following items will make your day more comfortable: Sunscreen – High SPF and in generous amounts; this river is famous for easily washing off "waterproof" sunscreens.

Which season should I come to the Nile? ▼

Water Levels:

The water levels here are very stable year-round because there is a dam built just after the Nile flows out of Lake Victoria. According to an agreement between the countries the Nile flows through, Lake Victoria must release a minimum flow of water to allow the Nile to flow into Egypt. This minimum flow creates excellent kayaking conditions. Generally, we have lower water levels in the morning and higher levels in the afternoon.

Seasons:

In recent years, the rainy seasons seem to have become a bit more unpredictable, but generally, the rainy season occurs twice a year: once from April to May, and another from October to December. Having said that, the sun still shines brightly during the rainy season, and it also still rains during the dry season! But one thing is for sure here: the weather is always warm!

Are there crocodiles or hippos? ▼

In the section of the Nile where we kayak (from Jinja to Busana), there are no hippos, and 99% of the crocodiles have been wiped out. Prior to Idi Amin's rule in the 1970s, these animals were abundant in the area, but since then, they have been completely poached out. After more than 20 years of rafting and kayaking activities, and hundreds of thousands of customers having fun on the Nile, there has never been a crocodile-related incident here.

If you want to see these animals on the Nile, you will need to drive 8 hours to Murchison Falls National Park.

Kayaking

Which kayak is best for me? ▼

Gentle, Calm & Relaxing

Our "Source of the Nile Tour," "Bujagali Lake Tour," and "Freedom Rentals" are designed for you. Calm waters, sunshine, bird watching, and perhaps a few drinks—these options are suited for those who want to spend a relaxing time on the river.

Whitewater Action

The Nile has some of the best whitewater in the world, making it one of the best environments in the world to learn kayaking. Those joining our tandem day trips or beginner courses usually have absolutely no prior kayaking experience; they are just eager for adventure, want to learn new skills, and want to see Uganda in a completely different way.

If you are still unsure, just drop us an email, and Wang or Witin will be very happy to advise you.

What should I wear? ▼

Wearing shorts, a T-shirt, and a swimsuit on the river is absolutely fine. We will provide all the necessary safety equipment for your activity. If you sunburn easily, we highly recommend wearing a long-sleeved shirt to cover your arms. We tend to not wear shoes when kayaking, or opt for thin neoprene boots, but if you want to wear something for the walk to and from the river, flip-flops or sneakers are a perfect choice.

Do I have to book in advance? ▼

Yes, for all our whitewater activities and the Source of the Nile Tour, booking is required. To avoid disappointment, we recommend booking at least 24 hours in advance. You can contact us via email, message us on WhatsApp, or if you are already in Uganda, please call +256 772 880 322 to make a booking.

Can I wear glasses while kayaking? ▼

Most people prefer wearing contact lenses rather than regular glasses when kayaking. If you only have regular glasses and must wear them, we highly recommend purchasing a non-slip glasses strap to ensure your glasses stay secured to your head. Whatever your choice, please make sure to bring spares just in case.

Do you have nose clips? ▼

Yes, we do. Nose clips are extremely useful for learning to roll and for keeping water out of your nose while heading down the rapids. You can ask us for one when checking in at the Bujagali office.

How do I get out of the kayak? ▼

Don't worry, getting out of our kayaks is very easy. We will teach you how to use a spray deck (a fabric that fits like a skirt to keep water out) and do some practice to ensure you are comfortable exiting the kayak.

Can I bring a camera? ▼

If you are joining our Source of the Nile Tour or Bujagali Lake Tour, then bringing a camera is a great idea. Your guide will carry a small waterproof box that can fit small digital cameras. For our whitewater trips, bringing a GoPro can be a lot of fun, and we have several helmets and kayaks already fitted with mounts. We do not recommend taking your phone or camera on the water, as there are almost no opportunities to use them during these trips.

Do I need to tip the guide? ▼

No, there is no expectation or strict rule to tip in Uganda. If you would like to tip your guide, we suggest you base the amount on the personal service you received and the appreciation you wish to show. A good reference point we use to gauge tipping when traveling around the world is: how much would it cost to buy a delicious meal for the person you want to tip? Here in Jinja, a great meal at a somewhat upscale restaurant costs around 35,000 Ugandan Shillings (approx. $8.92 USD / £7.04 GBP).

Travel & Visas

Can I get a Ugandan tourist visa at the border? ▼
As of February 2025, Uganda no longer issues visas on arrival for most foreign nationals, including tourists. All travelers entering Uganda, regardless of nationality, must obtain a visa prior to arrival.

e-Visa (Online):

The most efficient method is to apply online through the official Uganda visa website. E-visas are typically processed within 2-3 working days.

Visa Fees:

  • Single Entry Tourist Visa: $50 USD
  • East Africa Tourist Visa: $100 USD (allows travel to Uganda, Kenya, and Rwanda).
Is Uganda safe? ▼

Uganda is one of the friendliest and most peaceful countries in Africa. Petty and opportunistic crimes do occasionally happen, but serious crimes are extremely rare. As long as you exercise good travel common sense and keep your belongings secure, the risk of becoming a victim of crime is very low.

What kind of plug does Uganda use? ▼

Uganda uses "Type G" plugs and sockets, which are standard British plugs and sockets. Electricity can fluctuate, and we recommend using a surge protector whenever possible. The general specifications for power supply in Uganda are as follows: Voltage: 240V, Hertz: 50Hz, Plug Type: Type G.

How do I get online in Uganda? ▼

Getting online in Uganda is straightforward, with several options available: Buy a SIM card for mobile internet, at your hotel/guesthouse, at a dedicated internet shop, or at a cafe.

To get online with your unlocked phone, you can buy standard SIM, MicroSIM, and NanoSIM cards here. You will need to bring your passport (or other form of ID) when purchasing and registering the SIM card, but the whole process is very simple. 4G and Edge network coverage in the Jinja area is good.

What languages are spoken in Uganda? ▼

Uganda has about 50 local languages. English is the official language and is widely understood for basic communication. Most parts of central and southern Uganda speak variant languages derived from the Bantu language family, such as Luganda and Soga.

Health & Medical

Do I need a yellow fever vaccination card to go to Uganda? ▼

Yes; as of 2016, travelers must present a yellow fever vaccination certificate upon arriving in Entebbe. The World Health Organization announced that starting from July 11, 2016, existing and new yellow fever vaccination certificates are valid for life.

What vaccines do I need to go to Uganda? ▼

People traveling to Uganda are recommended to receive the following vaccinations. Before visiting Uganda, please consult your travel doctor to ensure you have sought professional medical advice. Recommended vaccinations: Diphtheria, Hepatitis A, Tetanus, Typhoid, Yellow Fever.

Malaria in Uganda ▼

Malaria is prevalent in Sub-Saharan Africa. For visitors traveling to Uganda, we recommend you take all necessary precautions, such as taking anti-malarial medication, wearing long pants and long sleeves in the evenings, and sleeping under a mosquito net at night. The most common malaria prevention medications in Uganda are Doxycycline and Malarone.

Will I get bilharzia/schistosomiasis in the Nile? ▼

It is widely known that the Nile carries bilharzia pathogens, and there are documented cases of infections from the river. Bilharzia is most prevalent in still and stagnant water. We spend most of our time in fast-flowing rapids, or on sections of the river where the water level fluctuates wildly every day, so these areas are essentially free of this parasite.

To minimize the risk, we recommend not spending time wading near the riverbanks, and avoiding swimming in the stagnant waters of Lake Victoria.

Can I drink the tap water? ▼

We do not recommend drinking tap water. During your time kayaking with us, we will provide bottled water. For general travel around Uganda, many villages have boreholes with pumps available to draw drinking water, and bottled water is very easily purchased in small shops and supermarkets.

Money & Payments

Are there ATMs in Jinja? ▼

There are many ATMs inside Jinja city, and we have found the most reliable ones at the following locations: Absa Bank, Stanbic Bank, I&M Bank, DTB Bank, and The Stanbic ATM located in Njeru.

Please note that there are daily withdrawal limits (depending on your bank), and you will be charged a processing fee for every withdrawal. There are no ATMs in Bujagali.

Uganda and US Dollars ▼

If you visit Uganda and choose to bring US Dollars, please bring notes issued from 2009 onwards. Most banks and currency exchange bureaus here do not accept US Dollars issued before 2009. To avoid having unusable US Dollars on hand, please make absolutely sure that all the US Dollars you bring are the 2009 version or newer.

Please note – across the country, notes with denominations lower than $50 will get a poorer exchange rate. Businesses, banks, and currency exchange bureaus frequently reject US Dollars that are marked, torn, or old. If you are traveling from the UK or Europe, consider bringing your GBP and Euros, as they are widely accepted.

Where can I exchange for Ugandan Shillings? ▼

The Ugandan Shilling is a restricted currency and can only be obtained within the East Africa region. You will not be able to get Ugandan Shillings outside of this region.

Alternatively, there are plenty of ATMs available upon arrival in Entebbe and in all major towns.

Jinja Town

Which restaurants do you recommend? ▼

We have been coming to Jinja for the past 10 years, and below are our favorite restaurants. Simply ask any boda (motorcycle taxi) driver for directions, and you can easily find all of these places.

  • Bacchus @ 30 Wilson: An excellent menu offering family favorites and delicious specials.
  • Moti Mahal: Jinja has several good Indian restaurants, but Moti Mahal is our favorite, and it's probably the restaurant with the best CP value in town.
  • The Deli: Main Street near the post office. Must-order: Coffee! And their smoothies!
  • The Hangout: A very cool spot, featuring multiple restaurants clustered in a group of container houses.
  • Zabuli: Run by a family from the US, offering classic American food.
  • The Source Café: One of the oldest establishments in Jinja, guaranteeing a taste of warm hospitality.
  • New Rendezvous: Although Uganda isn't renowned for its cuisine, among the Ugandan restaurants in Jinja, New Rendezvous is our favorite. Exceptionally high CP value.

Transport & Logistics

How do I get to you from Entebbe airport? ▼

For passengers flying in, we can arrange for a driver we trust to pick you and your luggage up from Entebbe (EBB) International Airport. This is cheaper than hailing a taxi at the airport, and the driver we send is someone we know who knows exactly where to take you. If needed, their roof racks can hold your kayak.

Current rates: Entebbe – Jinja / Bujagali – $80-$100 USD. The price is for a small car (5 seats, but with luggage, we recommend a maximum of 4 people) and includes a small booking fee.

Getting to Bujagali via Public Transport ▼

We strongly recommend that you charter a private car, as this makes better use of your time and is more convenient and safer. If you want to come via public transport, please follow these instructions:

Head to the Amber Court roundabout in Jinja – this roundabout is located on the main road between Kampala and Kenya, and your driver should know it. From the Amber Court roundabout, you need to head to Bujagali. The best way is to take a boda (motorcycle taxi); all the drivers should know where it is. The boda fare from the roundabout to the campsite is expected to be 7,000 or 10,000 Ugandan Shillings.

Independent Kayakers

How long should I stay on the Nile? ▼

We recommend planning a trip to Uganda for at least two weeks, though people are often still surprised by how fast time flies when it's over. Two weeks is enough time for you to experience everything on the river once, and perhaps squeeze in a trip to Murchison or Sipi Falls. Standard stay durations for whitewater kayakers coming here are two weeks, a month, and two months, sometimes even longer!

How do I handle meals if I stay in Bujagali village? ▼

No need to worry at all! In fact, you can absolutely stay here for the entire month without ever needing the hassle of traveling into Jinja city to find food. In Bujagali, you can truly taste 100% authentic local flavors!

There are small diners opened by locals in the village, where a filling, authentic meal only costs about 4,000 UGX. You absolutely cannot miss the classic street national food, "Chapati Rolex" (a Ugandan specialty omelet wrap), priced between 1,000 and 4,000 UGX—guaranteed to be delicious and satisfying! If you like to cook for yourself, small grocery stores can be seen everywhere in the village, stocking all daily ingredients.

How do I commute to and from the river? ▼

When kayaking on the Nile, transport shuttling is a daily routine. The local shuttle system is very mature; you just need to clearly tell the driver your put-in and take-out points directly.

Our exclusive highlight: Walk right to the river! From our partnered village accommodation complex, you can walk directly to the river! You can easily put in and take out at a fixed spot without needing to spend any time or money arranging a shuttle; just carry your boat over and you can immediately enjoy the Nile!

Do you rent kayaks/equipment? ▼

Yes, we do. We have a variety of kayaks, paddles, and soft equipment available for rent. We only rent kayaks to people we have taught before, or whose skills we have seen on the river. This is for your safety, and to ensure our equipment can be returned in good condition. Please contact us to get a list of available boats and to book the equipment you need during your time here.
